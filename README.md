SNHU CS320 Submission

## How can I ensure that my code, program, or software is functional and secure?
I ensure that my software is both functional and secure, by treating testing as continuous process rather than a final phase. It's something to be constantly reviewed and revised. In my coursework, I leveraged JUnit to create automated test suites that verified every requirement of my service classes. Coverage testing made sure that my JUnit tests would actually be effective with a high (80%+) coverage rate. I made sure that inputs are valid and not malformed, opting to throw errors rather than crash or accept possibly malicious code. Between that and the JUnit testing, I believe my code to be functional and secure.

##How do I interpret user needs and incorporate them into a program?
I approached user needs by acting as a translator between the goals and code. Breaking down broad stakeholder requests, I turn them into actionable user stories. Usually it ends up as a list of "the system must -". I've learned that the best way to incorporate user needs is through iterative feedback, ensuring that the user's goal is always driving how the program operates.

## How do I approach designing software?
My approach in design is based on modularity and maintainability. Throughout my coursework I tried to maintain the idea that setting the foundation correctly in the beginning is the most important part to having a functional and secure program. Keeping in mind the idea of technical debt, modularity lends itself best to a careful iterative process. In addition, a system designed to be easily iterated on is also more maintanable. The goal was to create discrete objects with logic that reduces the risk of causing a domino effect of issues.
